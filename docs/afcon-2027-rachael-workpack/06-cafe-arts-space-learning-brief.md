# Cafe Arts Space Learning Brief

## Purpose

This brief connects Rachael Nanyonjo's AFCON 2027 opportunity pathway to her long-term ambition of building a cafe arts space that combines hospitality, culture, performance, community, food, art, tourism and creative entrepreneurship.

AFCON should be treated not only as an event opportunity, but as a live learning laboratory for understanding how visitors move, spend, gather, eat, watch, listen, photograph, socialise and remember cultural experiences.

---

## Strategic Learning Question

How can a future cafe arts space become a visitor-friendly cultural hospitality venue that serves local communities, artists, tourists, diaspora guests and event audiences?

---

## What to Study During AFCON Preparation

### 1. Visitor Behaviour

Observe:

- Where visitors gather before and after matches.
- What food and drink they choose.
- How much they are willing to spend.
- Whether they prefer premium, casual or authentic local experiences.
- What safety, transport and payment concerns they have.
- What kind of music, art and atmosphere keeps them longer.

Questions:

- What would make a fan choose a cafe arts venue over a normal bar or restaurant?
- What would make diaspora guests feel culturally connected?
- What would make tourists feel safe and curious?

---

### 2. Venue Experience

Study cafes, hotels, restaurants, galleries, theatres, cultural centres and music venues.

Observe:

- Layout and seating.
- Lighting and sound.
- Menu structure.
- Pricing.
- Service speed.
- Toilets and cleanliness.
- Stage or performance area.
- Art display.
- Security.
- Parking and transport access.
- Social media visibility.

Output:

Create a venue observation sheet after each visit.

---

### 3. Event Programming

Study how venues programme events.

Observe:

- Frequency of events.
- Ticketing model.
- Free vs paid entry.
- Artist payment model.
- Theme nights.
- Open mic nights.
- Cultural nights.
- Food pairing events.
- Sponsor presence.
- Audience retention.

Possible cafe arts programming:

- Acoustic music nights.
- Poetry and storytelling evenings.
- Traditional-modern dance showcases.
- Film screening nights.
- Fashion and craft pop-ups.
- Diaspora cultural dinners.
- Artist talks.
- Football-and-culture watch parties.
- Youth performance showcases.

---

### 4. Tourism Partnerships

Study how tourism businesses package experiences.

Potential partners:

- Tour operators.
- Hotels.
- Airport transfer companies.
- Galleries.
- Museums.
- Craft markets.
- Restaurants.
- Cultural performers.
- Coffee producers.
- Diaspora networks.

Key question:

How can a cafe arts space become part of a visitor itinerary?

Possible products:

- 2-hour cultural evening.
- Ugandan food and music tasting.
- Coffee, art and storytelling session.
- Football fan cultural welcome.
- Diaspora homecoming dinner.
- Artist-led city walk ending at the cafe.

---

## Cafe Arts Space Business Pillars

### Pillar 1: Food and Drink

Menu should balance affordability, quality and cultural identity.

Ideas:

- Ugandan coffee.
- Tea and herbal infusions.
- Light Ugandan snacks.
- Fusion menu nights.
- Pre-event and post-event menus.

### Pillar 2: Art and Performance

The venue should have a clear cultural heartbeat.

Ideas:

- Rotating art exhibitions.
- Dance and movement showcases.
- Acoustic sessions.
- Storytelling nights.
- Theatre readings.
- Fashion pop-ups.

### Pillar 3: Tourism and Visitor Experience

The cafe should help visitors understand Uganda.

Ideas:

- Cultural maps.
- Short visitor guides.
- Tour operator partnerships.
- Event calendar.
- Diaspora welcome sessions.

### Pillar 4: Community and Youth

The space should build local talent.

Ideas:

- Youth rehearsals.
- Creative workshops.
- Mentorship sessions.
- Open stage nights.
- Skill-building clinics.

### Pillar 5: Creative Enterprise

The cafe should generate multiple income streams.

Potential revenue:

- Food and drinks.
- Ticketed events.
- Venue hire.
- Workshops.
- Merchandise.
- Art sales.
- Brand partnerships.
- Tourism packages.
- Memberships.

---

## AFCON Pilot Idea

### Cafe Arts Pop-Up: Football Meets Culture

A temporary pilot event hosted at an existing cafe, gallery, hotel lounge or cultural venue.

Components:

- Curated music.
- Short dance/movement performance.
- Ugandan food bites.
- Visual art display.
- Football-themed storytelling.
- Diaspora networking.
- Visitor feedback cards.

Purpose:

- Test the cafe arts model before investing in a full venue.
- Collect visitor feedback.
- Build portfolio evidence.
- Demonstrate cultural tourism potential.

## Reality Check

A cafe arts space should not be built only because AFCON is coming. AFCON can provide learning, visibility and pilot opportunities, but the long-term business must survive after the tournament through local customers, diaspora visitors, tourists, programming discipline, strong hospitality operations and careful cost control.
