# First 10 Contact Outreach Brief

Date prepared: 16 June 2026

Purpose: Convert the AFCON 2027 Rachael Nanyonjo workpack into the first controlled outreach sprint. This brief should be used together with `data/priority-contact-layer-phase-1.csv`, `10-message-bank-phase-1.md`, `02-formal-introduction-letter.md`, and `03-concept-note-afcon-cultural-experience.md`.

## Operating rule

Do not send mass messages. Each outreach should be tailored to the institution and should ask first for the correct department, referral, meeting, guidance, or pilot conversation. The goal is to open serious conversations, not to imply that any AFCON role has already been secured.

## Priority 1: FUFA

Reason for priority: FUFA is the official football gateway in Uganda and is the logical first route for referrals into AFCON 2027 local organising, fan engagement, media, development, ceremonies, or cultural programming structures.

First ask: Request a short introductory meeting or referral to the correct AFCON 2027 local organising / cultural / ceremonies / fan-experience contact.

Send: one-page profile, formal introduction letter, short concept note.

Tone: respectful, concise, official.

## Priority 2: Uganda Tourism Board

Reason for priority: UTB is the tourism-marketing and destination gateway. Rachael's angle should focus on visitor experience, cultural tourism, diaspora tourism, hotel/venue linkages and creative economy visibility.

First ask: Ask for the correct department or officer handling visitor experience, events, Meet in Uganda, partnerships, or destination-marketing opportunities linked to AFCON 2027.

Send: short WhatsApp/email first; attach full documents only after getting the correct recipient.

Tone: tourism-development and visitor-experience focused.

## Priority 3: KCCA

Reason for priority: Kampala is likely to carry major city pressure around visitors, fan movement, hospitality and public-facing cultural experiences. KCCA has city services, community services, Visit Kampala, events and public-space functions.

First ask: Request referral to events, community services, public-space activation, Visit Kampala, or cultural programming desk.

Send: intro email plus concept note only after identifying the correct directorate.

Tone: city visitor experience and creative-economy contribution.

## Priority 4: Ministry of Tourism, Wildlife and Antiquities

Reason for priority: Policy-level tourism, heritage and museums route. This contact is important for strategic legitimacy and understanding national tourism-readiness plans.

First ask: Request guidance or referral to Tourism Development / Museums and Monuments on how young or diaspora creatives can support cultural tourism readiness.

Send: formal letter and concept note after verifying current email/contact.

Tone: policy, heritage, tourism-sector learning.

## Priority 5: Uganda National Cultural Centre / National Theatre

Reason for priority: Core cultural and performance institution. Best route for stage, performance, choreography, cultural venue programming and youth mentorship concepts.

First ask: Request a meeting with programming or management team to discuss AFCON cultural experience collaboration and possible pilot programming.

Send: one-page profile, concept note, Rachael website link.

Tone: professional creative-sector partnership.

## Priority 6: Uganda Museum / Department of Museums and Monuments

Reason for priority: Heritage storytelling can strengthen visitor orientation and cultural-tourism routes during AFCON.

First ask: Request advice on cultural heritage routes, museum-linked visitor education, storytelling and possible visitor-orientation collaborations.

Send: short concept note and learning questions.

Tone: heritage, research, education and visitor-experience.

## Priority 7: Uganda Convention Bureau / Meet in Uganda

Reason for priority: AFCON will create side events, VIP hospitality, sponsor programmes and high-value visitor experiences. UCB may be relevant for MICE-style events and hosted visitor experiences.

First ask: Ask UTB for the correct UCB contact or route through the Meet in Uganda link.

Send: concept note after direct contact verification.

Tone: event tourism, hospitality and premium visitor experience.

## Priority 8: Uganda Wildlife Authority

Reason for priority: Visiting football fans, diaspora guests and sponsors may want safari or wildlife add-ons. Rachael's cultural tourism concept can connect fan experience with broader tourism itineraries.

First ask: Request referral to product development, tourism marketing, or visitor-experience team.

Send: only after official contact is verified.

Tone: tourism packaging and visitor add-on experiences.

## Priority 9: Kampala-Entebbe hotel cluster

Reason for priority: Hotels may move faster than government institutions. They will host fans, officials, sponsors, media and diaspora guests, and may need high-quality cultural entertainment.

First ask: Request a meeting with events manager / guest-experience manager about cultural evenings, VIP hospitality, or café-arts pop-up concepts during major football periods.

Send: one-page profile, concept note and sample run-of-show.

Tone: commercial, guest-experience, value-added hospitality.

## Priority 10: Event and creative agency cluster

Reason for priority: Large event companies and agencies may pitch official or sponsor activations. Rachael can be introduced as a creative consultant, movement director, choreography lead or cultural-programming collaborator.

First ask: Ask whether they are preparing AFCON 2027-related proposals and whether a creative-direction/choreography profile could be considered for collaboration.

Send: profile, website link and concept note.

Tone: peer-to-peer creative collaboration.
