# AFCON 2027 Rachael Nanyonjo Culture & Tourism Workpack

Prepared for: Byarugaba Henry Rwabushaija II  
Subject: Practical workpack for positioning Rachael Nanyonjo for AFCON 2027 culture, choreography, creative direction, event curation, tourism and cafe-arts legacy opportunities.

---

## Purpose

This workpack converts the AFCON 2027 Rachael Nanyonjo opportunity dossier into usable documents for outreach, meetings, proposals and implementation.

It is designed to support a realistic approach to AFCON 2027 opportunities in Uganda and the wider East African host region, especially across culture, choreography, artistic direction, fan-zone programming, hospitality-linked activations, tourism visitor experience, diaspora engagement and future cafe-arts space development.

---

## Core Strategy

Rachael should not be positioned as a beginner looking for any work. Her public profile supports a stronger senior-level framing: Ugandan-British international choreographer, director, movement director and creative consultant with the ability to support AFCON 2027 cultural programming, choreography, ceremony concepts, visitor experience and diaspora-facing creative tourism.

The recommended framing is:

> Rachael Nanyonjo can help Uganda and East Africa use AFCON 2027 as a cultural, tourism and creative-economy platform by contributing international-level creative direction, choreography, movement direction, fan-zone programming, ceremony concepts, diaspora cultural tourism and visitor-experience design.

## Recommended Use

Use the one-page profile and introduction letter for first contact. Use the concept note after someone shows interest. Use the outreach tracker weekly. Use the 30/90-day plan as the operating calendar. Use the technical toolkit only after a real event, pop-up, showcase or pilot activation becomes serious.

---

## Reality Check

This workpack does not claim that Rachael has secured AFCON work. It positions her professionally and gives a practical route to approach the right institutions, partners and private-sector actors.

All official AFCON operational opportunities must be verified through CAF, FUFA, government bodies, official local organising structures and credible institutional channels.
