# Meeting Questions and Script

## Purpose

This document prepares Rachael or Henry for meetings with tourism, culture, sports, hospitality, event, sponsor and diaspora contacts regarding possible AFCON 2027 culture and tourism opportunities.

The aim is to sound professional, useful and realistic. The meeting should not assume that work is already available. It should open a serious conversation and identify the correct channel.

---

## 1. Opening Script

Thank you very much for making time to speak with us.

We are exploring how Rachael Nanyonjo's creative background may support AFCON 2027 culture, choreography, visitor experience, hospitality activations, diaspora engagement and tourism-related programming.

Rachael is a Ugandan-British choreographer, director, movement director and creative consultant. The intention is not to bypass any official process, but to understand the correct planning channels and explore whether her experience could be useful to Uganda and the wider East African AFCON 2027 effort.

We would appreciate your guidance on where culture, tourism, creative programming and visitor experience are being coordinated, and whether there may be opportunities for collaboration, advisory input, volunteering, attachment, pilot activations, mentorship or formal engagement.

---

## 2. Short Personal Pitch

Rachael can be positioned as someone who can support:

- Cultural programming.
- Choreography and movement direction.
- Ceremony and fan-zone concepts.
- Hospitality-linked cultural activations.
- Diaspora creative tourism.
- Youth performance mentorship.
- Visitor experience design.
- Cafe-arts and cultural hospitality learning.

The long-term interest is also to understand how major sports events can help strengthen Uganda's tourism, hospitality and creative economy.

---

## 3. Questions for Official Football or Sports Contacts

1. Which office or committee is responsible for AFCON 2027 cultural programming, ceremonies or fan experience?
2. Will there be formal channels for creative consultants, choreographers, performers or cultural programmers?
3. Are there likely to be volunteer, supplier, consultancy or partnership opportunities?
4. How should someone with international choreography and movement direction experience submit a profile?
5. What should be avoided in order to respect official AFCON processes and branding rules?
6. Are there specific timelines for public calls, supplier registration or event planning?
7. Who would be the correct person to speak with next?

---

## 4. Questions for Tourism Bodies

1. What tourism opportunities are being developed around AFCON 2027?
2. How is Uganda preparing to convert incoming football visitors into tourism clients?
3. Will there be cultural tourism packages, city experiences or diaspora visitor programmes?
4. How can creative professionals support visitor experience?
5. Are there opportunities to shadow, intern, volunteer or support tourism preparation activities?
6. What skills are most needed in tourism and hospitality ahead of AFCON?
7. How can future cafe arts spaces serve tourists and diaspora visitors?

---

## 5. Questions for Culture Institutions

1. Are there plans for AFCON-related cultural programming?
2. How can dancers, choreographers, theatre makers, musicians and creative directors participate?
3. Is there a need for youth performance mentorship or choreography support?
4. Are there existing cultural groups that need professional direction or rehearsal structure?
5. Would a Uganda Cultural Welcome Night or Football Meets Culture showcase be useful?
6. Are there venues suitable for a pilot cultural activation?
7. Who approves or coordinates cultural programming linked to national events?

---

## 6. Questions for Hotels and Hospitality Partners

1. Are you expecting increased guest traffic during AFCON 2027?
2. Are you planning cultural evenings, live music, dance performances, diaspora receptions or VIP hospitality activations?
3. Would curated Ugandan cultural programming be useful for your guests?
4. Do you work with artists, performers or creative directors for guest experiences?
5. Would you consider hosting a small cafe-arts or cultural welcome pilot event?
6. What would make such an event commercially viable for the hotel?
7. Who handles programming, events or guest experience partnerships?

---

## 7. Questions for Event Companies and Creative Agencies

1. Are you preparing for AFCON-related client work?
2. Do you anticipate demand for choreography, creative direction, performers or cultural programming?
3. Would Rachael's international movement direction background be useful to your team?
4. How do you usually engage freelance creative consultants?
5. Can her profile be kept on file for future briefs?
6. Would there be room to collaborate on a cultural tourism concept?
7. What portfolio materials would you need from her?

---

## 8. Questions for Sponsors and Brands

1. Is your brand planning AFCON 2027-related activations?
2. Will culture, music, fashion, dance or visitor experience be part of your campaign?
3. Would a premium Uganda cultural welcome event or diaspora reception fit your brand goals?
4. Are you open to creative proposals from cultural professionals?
5. Which department handles sponsorship activations and experiential marketing?
6. What would make a cultural activation attractive to your brand?
7. What timelines should we be aware of?

---

## 9. Questions for Diaspora Networks

1. Are diaspora visitors expected to return for AFCON 2027?
2. What kind of cultural experiences would be meaningful for Ugandans abroad?
3. Would a diaspora creative reception or cafe-arts cultural evening be useful?
4. Which UK-Uganda or East African diaspora networks should be approached?
5. How can diaspora creatives collaborate with Uganda-based artists?
6. What would make the experience feel authentic and valuable?
7. Could Rachael help bridge UK and Ugandan creative networks?

---

## 10. Closing Script

Thank you again for your guidance.

Based on this conversation, we would be grateful if you could advise us on the correct next step. We are happy to share Rachael's one-page profile, formal concept note and any additional portfolio material required.

We do not want to assume any official role without the right process. Our goal is to understand where her creative experience can be useful and how she can contribute professionally to AFCON 2027 culture, tourism, hospitality and creative-sector legacy.
