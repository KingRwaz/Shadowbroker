# First Week Operating Note

Date prepared: 16 June 2026

This note tells the team what to do first with the new first-stage data layer.

## Objective for week one

Convert the Rachael Nanyonjo AFCON 2027 cultural tourism concept into real conversations with verified institutions and partners.

The week-one goal is not to secure a job immediately. The goal is to identify the right people, verify contact routes, send the first professional introductions, and book initial conversations.

## Files to use first

1. `data/priority-contact-layer-phase-1.csv`
   Use this as the first stakeholder map. It separates verified public details from items that still require manual confirmation.

2. `12-first-10-contact-outreach-brief.md`
   Use this to understand why each target matters and what the first ask should be.

3. `data/contact-verification-queue-phase-1.csv`
   Use this to assign verification tasks before sending sensitive or formal material.

4. `10-message-bank-phase-1.md`
   Use this for WhatsApp, email, referral and follow-up messages.

5. `02-formal-introduction-letter.md`
   Use this when the correct person or department has been confirmed.

6. `03-concept-note-afcon-cultural-experience.md`
   Send this only after the recipient has shown interest or requested more detail.

## Week-one daily sequence

### Day 1: Verify priority contacts

Start with FUFA, Uganda Tourism Board, KCCA, Uganda National Cultural Centre and the Ministry of Tourism, Wildlife and Antiquities.

Confirm names, titles, phone numbers, emails and preferred submission route.

Do not guess email addresses.

### Day 2: Send first three introductions

Send tailored first messages to FUFA, UTB and KCCA.

Ask for the correct department or a short introductory meeting.

Do not attach too many files at first unless the contact route is formal and verified.

### Day 3: Prepare cultural-sector route

Verify Uganda National Cultural Centre / National Theatre and Uganda Museum contacts.

Prepare a version of the pitch focused on choreography, cultural programming, performance, heritage storytelling and visitor experience.

### Day 4: Prepare hospitality route

Begin a list of 10 Kampala-Entebbe hotels and 5 cafe/gallery venues.

Call or visit to identify events managers, guest-experience managers or programming contacts.

### Day 5: Prepare agency route

Identify 10 event companies, production companies or experiential marketing agencies.

Ask whether they are preparing AFCON 2027-related pitches or sports/culture activations.

### Day 6: Follow up and record everything

Update the outreach tracker, contact layer and verification queue.

Record who replied, who referred, who requested documents and who needs another follow-up.

### Day 7: Review and decide next moves

Review the week's results.

Choose the next 10 contacts.

Update the concept note based on feedback.

Prepare for any booked meetings.

## Minimum evidence to build in week one

- 10 verified contact records.
- 3 first messages sent.
- 2 follow-ups scheduled.
- 1 confirmed meeting or referral target.
- 1 updated profile package.
- 1 clear list of missing information.

## Conduct rules

Be respectful and concise.

Never claim an AFCON role has been secured.

Never pay an intermediary for vague access.

Never send confidential personal information to unverified contacts.

Always record the source of every phone number, email and name.

Start with access and guidance; convert to collaboration only after trust is built.
